Whale Watching Tips

March is prime time for Gray Whale migration. Here's how to make it great for Ada:

Bring Binoculars

While whales often come close, binoculars help Ada see the "blow" from a distance.

Motion Comfort

Consider non-drowsy seasickness options for kids if the water is choppy.

Warm Layers

It's always 10-15 degrees colder on the water. Bring a cozy jacket!

Tide Pool Explorers

Interaction Rule:

Look, don't touch! This is a protected reserve. No shell collecting allowed.

What to look for with Ada:

Crabs & Anemones

Check the crevices for shore crabs. Look for "squuishy" sea anemones—they look like flowers under the water!

Nudibranchs

If you're lucky, you'll spot a bright purple or orange sea slug. They are tiny but beautiful!

**Wear water shoes!** The rocks at Dike Rock are very slippery with algae. No flip-flops.

Bird Rock Waterfront Walk

Located just south of La Jolla, this is a more tranquil coastal experience.

  • **Calumet Park:** Best spot for a bluff-top family photo with the ocean background.
  • **The Walk:** The paved path is stroller/kid friendly and offers great views of surfers.
